Choose your frame

Choosing your frame is an important step in deciding how and what you would like to use your drone for. Each size has its unique advantages and caters to different pilot needs and skill levels. Your choice should align with your primary use-case, whether it's for fun, racing, hobbyist photography, or professional aerial imaging.

3 Inch Frames
These are the smallest in this range and are known for their exceptional agility and speed. Ideal for drone racing and acrobatics, they are adept at quick maneuvers and flying in tight spaces. Their compact size makes them easily portable and great for pilots who enjoy a high-octane flying experience. However, their small size limits battery capacity and payload, making them less suitable for photography or videography.

4 Inch Frames
Offering a balance between agility and stability, 4-inch frames are versatile. They are still agile enough for racing and acrobatic flying but start to offer more stability for smoother flight patterns. These frames can carry slightly larger batteries than 3-inch frames, leading to longer flight times. They are suitable for pilots who want a mix of fun flying and the potential for basic photography.

5 Inch Frames
This size is among the most popular for drone enthusiasts. 5-inch drones strike a balance between agility, speed, and stability. They have enough space to carry decent-sized batteries for extended flight times and can support better cameras than smaller drones. They are well-suited for both amateur and professional uses, including intermediate-level racing, freestyle flying, and entry-level aerial photography.

6 Inch Frames
These frames are leaning more towards stability and payload capacity. They are great for aerial photography and videography, offering smoother flights and the ability to carry more sophisticated camera systems. Their larger size and weight make them less nimble but provide better resistance to wind. They are ideal for pilots interested in capturing high-quality aerial footage or engaging in long-range flights.
Choose a 6 Inch Frame

7 Inch + Frames
These are the largest in the range and are best for specialized applications like professional-grade aerial photography, videography, and long-range exploration. They offer the highest stability, longest flight times, and the capacity to carry heavy and advanced camera equipment. While they lack the agility of smaller frames, their ability to maintain steady flight in various conditions makes them the go-to choice for commercial and professional drone pilots.
